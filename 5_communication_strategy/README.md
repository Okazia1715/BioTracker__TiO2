# Communication Strategy

## Target Audience, Capabilities, and Intended Impact

### 1. Target Audience and Context

The target audience for the project includes biologists or life
science students dealing with image analysis of microscopy images
or quantitative analysis of cell structures. This includes
undergraduate or graduate students, researchers pursuing a PhD in
life sciences, or technicians in the field of biology or life
sciences. They are usually dealing with PNG or TIFF image files of
microscopy images. They are usually required to perform segmentation
of cells or quantitative analysis of particles inside or outside the
cell. They are usually using image segmentation tools like ImageJ
or Fiji to perform the required analysis. They are usually required
to export the data to Microsoft Excel for performing statistical
analysis. They are usually experts in their field of life sciences
but are not experts in machine learning, Python, or environment
setup.

### 2. Capabilities and Constraints of the Audience

The target audience is capable of carrying out experimental design,
microscopy acquisition, and manual image annotation. They are
familiar with adhering to step-by-step instructions and installing
software when the instructions are provided properly. The audience
is able to comprehend overlays, understand the meaning of
segmentation masks, and work with CSV files.

However, there are important audience constraints when it comes to
interacting with the computer program. From a technical standpoint,
the audience might not be familiar with command-line interfaces,
Python packages, GPU environments, and training neural networks. In
addition, there might be IT-related constraints from the
institution’s IT department regarding the installation of software
and administrative access.

Another important audience constraint is the psychological factor.
Most biologists are not comfortable with the “black box AI” system
and do not wish to give up control of their data. There is also the
fear of the computer program changing the original images and
introducing biases into the data. Therefore, trust is an important
factor in the audience communication strategy.

### 3. User Personas

To better understand the people I am designing for, I have come up
with two user personas.

Maria is a 28-year-old PhD student working on her dissertation about
the accumulation of nanoparticles in colon tissues. She has
collected hundreds of fluorescence microscopy images and still
processes the data using the ImageJ plugin to do the segmentation
manually. The process is time-consuming and sometimes feels
subjective. She is in need of statistics that are reproducible and
quantitative enough to be used in publications. She does not have
the time or the desire to write her own Python script. Her biggest
concern is the integrity of the raw experimental data.

Alex is a 23-year-old student who is starting his master’s degree
and is working on his master’s project about image analysis using
computer vision techniques. He is familiar with the name “Cellpose”
and is concerned about the command-line process and the dependencies
required to run the program. He is looking for a program with a
graphical user interface where he can use the trained model without
the need to write his own code.

Both of the personas represent very motivated people who need
automation but still value clarity, safety, and scientific
credibility.

### 5. Intended Learning Outcome

After engaging with the communication artifact, the audience should
take away the following: AI-based cell segmentation is open,
repeatable, and accessible even if they don’t know fancy
programming. They should see how the graphical interface can make
deep learning more efficient without compromising scientific rigor.
Most importantly, they should understand how the original image data
remains unchanged and how every output is generated as separate
files with documentation. The audience should also understand how
the process of quantitative analysis reduces bias and increases
reproducibility between experiments.

### 6 Communication Strategy

My approach will be centered on three main concepts: visual
clarity, openness, and simplicity. First, I have to consider the
fact that to a biologist, pictures are everything. Therefore, I will
ensure that I provide a certain level of clarity in the way I
present the image segmentation, the particle maps, and the
resulting statistical tables. I will also ensure that I provide a
before-and-after image so that the viewer can be less skeptical
about the whole process.

The second concept that I have to consider in my approach is
openness. I will ensure that I am open to the viewer by clearly
indicating that the images are not manipulated in any way. I will
also ensure that I present the whole process in a way that it is not
a black box to the viewer. I will present it in a step-by-step
